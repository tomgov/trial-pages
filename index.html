Deals for schools

Information on the national deals that are available to schools to help them save money on some of the things they buy regularly.

Open allClose all

There are 6 main deals available at the moment.

New deals are being negotiated all the time, revisit this page regularly to see when others have been added.

Type of deal Potential benefits available to schools
Energy and water supplies 10% saving on schools’ utility bills.
Loans to fund energy saving schemes An interest-free loan to help schools make energy-saving improvements to their premises.
Printers, photocopiers or scanners 40% saving for individual schools’ photocopier and printer costs.
Microsoft software licensing Savings and/or better licensing terms on Microsoft products
ICT for education Easy-to-use deal with pre-negotiated prices from suppliers specialising in ICT for education settings.
Risk protection arrangement An alternative to commercial insurance that’s underwritten by government and is easy for schools to opt-in to.

Energy and water supplies

National deals are available for electricity and gas, in the first year primary schools could save more than £1,265 and secondary schools could save more than £8,000 on their energy bills.

Schools can also get better value for money on their water supply and sewerage services by buying services for all their premises from one single supplier (without any area or regional restrictions). This makes billing simpler, saving time and administration costs.

The savings schools make on their energy bills can also be used to pay off an interest free loan that we’ve negotiated to enable schools to fund energy saving schemes in their premises.

Who can get the deal: all publicly funded schools, including academies and free schools.

More information: read the Crown Commercial Service (CCS) information about energy deals for schools.

Take a look at the water deal and find out how you could save money.

Energy efficiency loans for schools

This is an interest free loan provided to schools by Salix, a not-for-profit organisation sponsored by the Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy.

Schools can use the money to carry out energy saving improvements to their premises, including insulation and LED lighting. Schools can pay back the money entirely from savings they make on their energy bills.

The scheme has helped yield around £16 million of savings for schools since 2014.

Who can get the deal: all local-authority-maintained schools and grant-aided schools.

More information: details of the loans and how to apply for them are available on the Salix website.

Photocopiers, printers and scanners

This deal enables schools to purchase photocopiers, printers and scanners (multi-functional devices, MFDs) at a nationally negotiated rate. With schools reporting savings of around 40% for their printer and photocopying costs.

This is a flexible agreement that allows schools to purchase certain items directly from a supplier at a competitive price, pre-negotiated by Crown Commercial Service (CCS). Alternatively schools can run their own mini competition if they have specific requirements that need to be met.

All suppliers offer excellent service and quick fault response call-out times.

Who can get the deal: all schools and publicly funded institutions.

More information: read the Crown Commercial Service MFD framework and use it to purchase new equipment.

Microsoft software

The Microsoft software memorandum of understanding (MoU) is an agreement that allows UK schools to buy or licence a wide range of Microsoft software with discounts of around 80% off recommended retail prices and/or better licensing terms.

Who can get the deal: all schools.

More information: read the Microsoft memorandum of understanding and find out how your school can take advantage.

Information and communication technology (ICT) for education

This agreement provides schools and colleges with a simple and flexible way to purchase IT services, hardware and related infrastructure to meet their individual needs. Crown Commercial Service (CCS) have selected the suppliers because they specialise in providing ICT services and solutions for education settings.

Schools can buy services by running a simple mini-competition with the support of Crown Commercial Services.

Who can get the deal: all schools, academies, universities and colleges.

More information: read the Crown Commercial Service ICT for schools framework and use it to purchase new technology.

Risk protection arrangement for academy trusts

The risk protection arrangement (RPA) is an alternative to commercial insurance for academies, that’s underwritten by the government. Over 61% of academies have already signed up.

Schools can easily opt into the arrangement and the Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA) will deduct the cost, £20 per pupil, directly from their general annual grant.

Who can get the deal: all academy trusts, including free schools.

More information: read the RPA membership rules which include a summary of cover.

The academies RPA guidance explains how to opt in.
